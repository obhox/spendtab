# SpendTab Blog Content Creation Guide for AI Agents

## Purpose

This guide defines the rules, structure, and standards for generating SEO-optimised blog posts for the SpendTab platform. Every piece of content produced must follow these instructions precisely. The goal is to rank on search engines, drive organic traffic from small business owners and entrepreneurs, and convert readers into SpendTab users.

---

## Content Philosophy

Every blog post exists to educate the target audience — small business owners, freelancers, and entrepreneurs — while naturally positioning SpendTab as the tool that solves their financial management problems. Content must feel authoritative, helpful, and practical. It should never feel like an advertisement. The product mention must arise organically from the context of the topic being discussed.

Write like an expert explaining a concept to a peer. Be direct, confident, and clear. Never condescend. Never pad the article with filler. Every sentence must earn its place.

---

## Audience Profile

The primary reader is a small business owner or freelancer who manages their own finances, may not have a dedicated accountant, and is actively looking for ways to save time, reduce errors, and grow their business. They are time-poor, results-oriented, and skeptical of generic advice. They want specific, actionable guidance that speaks to their real-world situation.

Secondary readers include startup founders, e-commerce sellers, and independent contractors who are scaling up and starting to feel the pain of unorganised financial records.

## SEO Fundamentals Every Article Must Follow

### Target Keyword Strategy

Each article is built around one primary keyword and two to four secondary keywords. The primary keyword must appear in the title, the first paragraph, at least one H2 subheading, and the meta description. Secondary keywords should appear naturally throughout the body without forcing them. Keyword stuffing is forbidden. Aim for a keyword density of one to two percent for the primary keyword.

Choose keywords with strong commercial or informational intent. Topics that serve small business owners searching for financial advice, tools, or how-to guidance are ideal. Examples of strong keyword clusters include "small business expense tracking," "cash flow management for freelancers," "how to manage business finances," and "invoicing software for small businesses."

### Title Tag and Headline

The H1 title must be between fifty and sixty characters. It must include the primary keyword close to the beginning. It should be compelling and create curiosity or communicate a clear benefit. Avoid clickbait. Avoid vague titles like "Everything You Need to Know." Instead, be specific: what the reader will learn, achieve, or understand by the end.

### Meta Description

Write a meta description between one hundred fifty and one hundred sixty characters. It must include the primary keyword, communicate the article's value clearly, and end with a subtle call to action or a statement of benefit. The meta description does not appear in the markdown body of the article but must be included as a frontmatter comment or clearly labelled section at the top of the document.

### URL Slug

Include a recommended URL slug in the frontmatter. It should be lowercase, hyphen-separated, and contain the primary keyword. Keep it under sixty characters. Remove stop words like "a," "the," and "how."

### Internal and Contextual Linking

Each article should mention SpendTab at least two to three times in a way that is contextually relevant. At least one mention should include a direct reference to a specific SpendTab feature, such as expense categorisation, invoice generation, budget tracking, or multi-account management. These mentions should feel like natural recommendations, not forced promotions.

## Article Structure

### Frontmatter Block

Every article must begin with a frontmatter block formatted as follows:

```
---
title: [Article Title — 50 to 60 characters]
description: [150 to 160 characters including primary keyword]
date: [YYYY-MM-DD]
author: SpendTab Team
authorTitle: Finance & Business Growth
category: [Finance Tips | Tax & Compliance | Business Growth]
tags: [tag one, tag two, tag three]
---
```

Do not include a `coverImage` or `coverAlt` field. The blog does not use cover images.

### Opening Section — Hook and Context

The first section has no subheading. It begins immediately after the frontmatter. The opening paragraph must include the primary keyword within the first one hundred words. It must hook the reader by acknowledging a real pain point, stating a surprising fact, or asking a question that reflects the reader's own situation. It must communicate what the article will cover and why the reader should keep reading.

This section should be two to three paragraphs. The final paragraph of this section should transition smoothly into the first H2.

### Body Sections — H2 Headings

The body of the article is divided into four to six major sections, each introduced by an H2 heading. Each H2 section contains two to four paragraphs. Each section must advance the topic and add genuine value. Do not repeat information across sections. Each section should feel like a natural progression from the last.

At least one H2 section must include a secondary keyword in its heading. Subheadings improve readability and signal content structure to search engines. Use them meaningfully, not decoratively.

Within H2 sections, H3 subheadings may be used to break down complex topics into digestible parts. H3s are optional but encouraged when a section covers more than one distinct sub-topic.

### SpendTab Integration Points

SpendTab should be referenced in at least two separate sections of the body. The reference must tie directly to the section's topic. For example, if a section covers expense tracking, mention how SpendTab's expense categorisation and account management features make the process faster and less error-prone. If a section covers invoicing, reference SpendTab's invoice generation capability.

Avoid generic phrases like "a tool like SpendTab can help." Instead, be specific. Describe what the reader would actually do inside SpendTab, how it changes their workflow, or what outcome they can expect. Position SpendTab as the practical answer to the problem the section is discussing.

### Closing Section — Conclusion and Call to Action

The final section uses an H2 heading such as "Taking the Next Step" or a variation appropriate to the article topic. It summarises the key takeaway in one to two paragraphs without repeating the body verbatim. It reinforces the article's central argument. The final paragraph must include a natural call to action that directs the reader toward SpendTab. This should not be aggressive or salesy. It should feel like a logical next step — "if you want to put these practices into action, SpendTab gives you the tools to do it without the overhead."

## Writing Style and Tone Rules

### No Em Dashes

Do not use em dashes (—) anywhere in the article body. Rewrite any sentence that would naturally use one. Use a comma, a full stop, or restructure the sentence entirely. Em dashes interrupt reading flow and are inconsistent with the plain, direct tone this blog requires.

### No Bullet Points or Lists

This is a strict rule with no exceptions. All information must be communicated in full sentences and paragraphs. Lists break reading flow, reduce time on page, and signal low-effort content to search engines. If you find yourself wanting to list items, restructure the content into a paragraph with transitional phrases such as "first," "in addition," "another critical factor," and "finally."

### Paragraph Length and Readability

Paragraphs should be three to five sentences on average. Short one-sentence paragraphs are acceptable for emphasis but should not be overused. Long paragraphs beyond six sentences should be split. Sentence length should vary — short sentences for impact, longer sentences for explanation. Flesch reading ease should target a score of sixty or above. Write at a level that is clear to a non-technical business owner.

### Voice and Perspective

Write in second person ("you," "your business") to create a direct connection with the reader. Avoid first person ("we think," "in our opinion") unless attributing a specific SpendTab perspective. Avoid passive voice wherever possible. Active voice is more authoritative and easier to read.

### Word Count

Every article must fall within the one thousand five hundred to two thousand word range, excluding frontmatter. Do not publish articles shorter than one thousand four hundred words or longer than two thousand one hundred words. Use the structure of the article — number of H2 sections and paragraph depth — to control length naturally.

---

## Quality Checks Before Finalising

Every article must pass the following checks before it is considered complete.

The primary keyword appears in the title, first paragraph, at least one H2, and the meta description. The word count falls between one thousand five hundred and two thousand words. No bullet points or numbered lists appear anywhere in the body. SpendTab is mentioned at least twice in the body with specific, contextually relevant references. Each H2 section contains at least two full paragraphs. The closing section includes a natural call to action pointing to SpendTab. The frontmatter is complete with all required fields filled in. The tone is direct, helpful, and non-promotional. No filler phrases appear such as "in today's fast-paced world," "it goes without saying," or "needless to say."

---

## Topic Selection Criteria

Topics must match one of the following intent categories. Informational intent topics teach the reader something they are actively searching for, such as "how to track business expenses" or "what is cash flow management." Commercial investigation topics help the reader compare options or understand what tools exist, such as "best invoicing tools for freelancers." Problem-aware topics address a specific pain the reader is experiencing, such as "why your small business is always short on cash."

Avoid topics that are too broad to rank or too niche to attract meaningful search volume. Each topic should have a realistic chance of appearing on the first page of search results for readers with genuine business finance needs. Topics should align with the features and value proposition of SpendTab, ensuring that the product mention is always relevant rather than forced.

---

## Final Note to the AI Agent

Your job is not to write about SpendTab. Your job is to write about the reader's problem, and to make SpendTab the obvious answer. Every article you produce must leave the reader with three things: a clear understanding of the topic, practical steps they can take immediately, and an awareness that SpendTab exists to make those steps easier. Write with authority. Write with clarity. Write to help.
